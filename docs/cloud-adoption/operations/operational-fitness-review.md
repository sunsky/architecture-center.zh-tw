# <a name="establishing-an-operational-fitness-review"></a><span data-ttu-id="d415c-103">建立操作適用性檢閱</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-103">Establishing an operational fitness review</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-104">當您的企業開始在 Azure 中處理工作負載時，下一步是建立**操作適用性檢閱**程序以列舉、實作及反覆檢閱這些工作負載的**非功能性**需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-104">As your enterprise begins to operate workloads in Azure, the next step is to establish an **operational fitness review** process to enumerate, implement, and iteratively review the **non-functional** requirements for these workloads.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-105">「非功能性」需求與服務的預期操作行為相關。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-105">_Non-functional_ requirements are related to the expected operational behavior of the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-106">非功能性需求有五個基本類別，稱為[軟體品質要素](../../guide/pillars.md)：延展性、可用性、復原能力 (包括商業持續性和災害復原)、管理與安全性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-106">There are five essential categories of non-functional requirements referred to as the [pillars of software quality](../../guide/pillars.md): scalability, availability, resiliency (including business continuity and disaster recovery), management, and security.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-107">操作適用性檢閱程序的目的是確保您的任務關鍵工作負載在考慮品質要素的情況下符合您公司的預期。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-107">The purpose of an operational fitness review process is ensuring that your mission critical workloads meet the expectations of your business with respect to the quality pillars.</span></span>

## <a name="understanding-the-problem"></a><span data-ttu-id="d415c-114">了解問題</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-114">Understanding the problem</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-115">如同您在[開始使用](../../cloud-adoption/getting-started/overview.md)所學到的，企業數位轉型的第一步是找出可透過採用 Azure 來解決的公司問題。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-115">As you learned in [Getting started](../../cloud-adoption/getting-started/overview.md), the first step in an enterprise's digital transformation is identifying the business problems to be solved by adopting Azure.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-116">下一步是粗略判斷問題的解決方案，例如將工作負載移轉到雲端，或採用現有的內部部署服務來獲得雲端功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-116">The next step is to determine a high-level solution to the problem, such as migrating a workload to the cloud, or adapting an existing on-premises service to include cloud functionality.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-117">最後，必須設計並實作解決方案。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-117">Finally, the solution is designed and implemented.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-118">在此程序期間，焦點通常是服務的「功能」。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-118">During this process, the focus is often on the _features_ of the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-119">也就是說，要讓服務可執行，必須滿足一些「功能」需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-119">That is, there are a set of desired _functional_ requirements for the service to perform.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-120">例如，產品交貨服務需要可用來判斷產品來源與目的地位置、在交貨期間追蹤產品、客戶通知等功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-120">For example, a product delivery service requires features for determining the source and destination locations of the product, tracking the product during delivery, customer notifications, and others.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-121">相反地，「非功能性」需求與一些屬性相關，例如服務的[可用性](../../checklist/availability.md)、[復原能力](../../resiliency/index.md)與[延展性](../../checklist/scalability.md)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-121">In contrast, the _non-functional_ requirements relate to properties such as the service's [availability](../../checklist/availability.md), [resiliency](../../resiliency/index.md), and [scalability](../../checklist/scalability.md).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-122">這些屬性與功能性需求不同，因為它們不會直接影響服務中任何特定功能的最終功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-122">These properties differ from the functional requirements because they do not directly affect the final function of any particular feature in the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-123">不過，這些非功能性需求與服務的「效能」與「持續性」相關。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-123">However, these non-functional requirements are related to the _performance_ and _continuity_ of the service.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-124">某些非功能性需求能以服務等級協定 (SLA) 的方式來指定。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-124">Some non-functional requirements can be specified in terms of a service level agreement (SLA).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-125">例如，與服務持續性相關時，服務的可用性需求能以如**可用時間 99.99%** 的百分比來表示。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-125">For example, with regard to service continuity, an availability requirement for the service can be expressed as a percentage such as **available 99.99% of the time**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-126">其他非功能性需求可能較難定義，而且可能會在產品需求發展時變更。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-126">Other non-functional requirements may be more difficult to define and may change as production needs evolve.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-127">例如，客戶面向的服務在人氣暴增之後可能必須開始面對意料之外的輸送量。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-127">For example, a consumer-facing service might start facing unanticipated throughput requirements after a surge of popularity.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-128">![注意] [為 Azure 設計具復原能力的應用程式](../../resiliency/index.md#define-your-availability-requirements)中深入探討如何定義復原能力的需求 
(包括 RPO、RTO、SLA 與相關概念的解釋)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-128">![NOTE] Defining the requirements for resiliency, including explanations of RPO, RTO, SLA, and related concepts, are explored in more depth in [Designing resilient applications for Azure](../../resiliency/index.md#define-your-availability-requirements).</span></span>

## <a name="operational-fitness-review-process"></a><span data-ttu-id="d415c-129">操作適用性檢閱程序</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-129">Operational fitness review process</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-130">維持企業服務效能與持續性的關鍵是實作「操作適用性檢閱」程序。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-130">The key to maintaining the performance and continuity of an enterprise's services is to implement an _operational fitness review_ process.</span></span>

![操作適用性檢閱程序的概觀](_images/ofr-flow.png)

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-132">從高階觀點來看，該程序有兩個階段。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-132">At a high level, the process has two phases.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-133">在先決條件階段，會建立需求並對應到支援的服務。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-133">In the prerequisites phase, the requirements are established and mapped to supporting services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-134">這比較少發生；可能每年發生一次或當導入新作業時發生。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-134">This occurs less frequently; perhaps annually or when new operations are introduced.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-135">先決條件階段的輸出會在流程階段中使用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-135">The output of the prerequisites phase is used in the flow phase.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-136">流程階段較常發生；建議的頻率是每個月一次。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-136">The flow phase occurs more frequently; we recommend monthly.</span></span>

### <a name="prerequisites-phase"></a><span data-ttu-id="d415c-137">先決條件階段</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-137">Prerequisites phase</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-138">此階段中的步驟旨在擷取針對重要服務進行定期檢閱的必要需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-138">The steps in this phase are intended to capture the necessary requirements for conducting a regular review of the important services.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d415c-139">**找出關鍵商業作業**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-139">**Identify critical business operations**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-140">找出企業的**任務關鍵**商業作業。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-140">Identify the enterprise's **mission critical** business operations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-141">商業作業與任何支援的服務功能不同。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-141">Business operations are independent from any supporting service functionality.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-142">換句話說，商業作業代表公司必須執行並由 IT 服務集提供支援的實際活動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-142">In other words, business operations represent the actual activities that the business needs to perform and are supported by a set of IT services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-143">「任務關鍵」 (或稱為「商業關鍵」)一詞反映出實作作業時會對公司造成的重大影響。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-143">The term _mission critical_, or alternatively _business critical_, reflects a severe impact to the business if the operation is impeded.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-144">例如，線上零售商可能會有「讓客戶將項目加到購物車」或「處理信用卡付款」的商業作業。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-144">For example, an online retailer may have a business operation such as "enable a customer to add an item to a shopping cart" or "process a credit card payment".</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-145">若任一作業失敗，客戶將無法完成交易，而且公司將無法實現銷售。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-145">If either of these operations were to fail, a customer would be unable to complete the transaction and the enterprise would fail to realize sales.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d415c-146">**將作業對應到服務**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-146">**Map operations to services**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-147">將這些商業作業對應到支援它們的服務。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-147">Map these business operations to the services that support them.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-148">在上面的購物車範例中，可能涉及多個服務：庫存管理服務、購物車服務等。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-148">In the above shopping cart example, several services may be involved: an inventory stock management service, a shopping cart service, and others.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-149">在上面的信用卡付款範例中，內部部署付款服務可能會與第三方付款處理服務互動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-149">In the credit card payment example above, an on-premises payment service may interact with a third-party payment processing service.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d415c-150">**分析服務相依性**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-150">**Analyze service dependencies**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-151">大部分的商業作業都需要多個支援服務或在多個支援服務之間進行協調。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-151">Most business operations require orchestration between multiple supporting services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-152">請務必了解服務與透過這些服務完成之任務關鍵交易流程之間的相依性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-152">It is important to understand the dependences between the services and the flow of mission critical transactions through these services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-153">您也應該考慮內部部署服務與 Azure 服務之間的相依性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-153">You should also consider the dependencies between on-premises services and Azure services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-154">在購物車範例中，庫存管理服務可能是裝載在內部部署環境中，並內嵌來自實體倉儲之員工的資料輸入，但它可能會將資料儲存在 [Azure 儲存體](/azure/storage/common/storage-introduction)之類的 Azure 服務中或 [Azure Cosmos DB](/azure/cosmos-db/introduction) 之類的資料庫中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-154">In the shopping cart example, the inventory stock management service may be hosted on-premises and ingest data input by employees from a physical warehouse, but it may store data in an Azure service such as [Azure storage](/azure/storage/common/storage-introduction) or a database such as [Azure Cosmos DB](/azure/cosmos-db/introduction).</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-155">這些活動的輸出是一組服務作業的**計分卡計量**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-155">An output from these activities is a set of **scorecard metrics** for service operations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-156">計量是以非功能性條件的方式分類，例如可用性、延展性與災害復原。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-156">The metrics are categorized in terms of non-functional criteria such as availability, scalability, and disaster recovery.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-157">計分卡計量表示服務預期符合作業需求的條件。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-157">Scorecard metrics express the criteria that the service is expected to meet operationally.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-158">這些計量能以適用於服務作業的任何層級細微性表示。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-158">These metrics can be expressed at any level of granularity that is appropriate for the service operation.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-159">計分卡應該以在公司擁有者與工程人員之間促使有意義之決策的簡單方式表示。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-159">The scorecard should be expressed in simple terms to facilitate meaningful discussion between the business owners and engineering.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-160">例如，延展性計分卡計量可能以下列方式表示：「綠色」代表以預期條件執行、「黃色」代表無法符合預期條件但主動實作規劃的補救措施，而「紅色」代表無法符合預期條件且未採取任何計畫或動作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-160">For example, a scalability scorecard metric could be expressed as _green_ for performing at the desired criteria, _yellow_ for failing to meet the desired criteria but actively implementing a planned remediation, and _red_ for failing to meet the desired criteria with no plan or action.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-161">務必強調這些計量應該直接反映商業需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-161">It is important to emphasize that these metrics should directly reflect business needs.</span></span>

### <a name="service-review-phase"></a><span data-ttu-id="d415c-162">服務檢閱階段</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-162">Service review phase</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d415c-163">服務檢閱階段是操作適用性檢閱程序的核心。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-163">The service review phase is core of the operational fitness review process.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d415c-164">**監視服務計量**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-164">**Measure service metrics**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-165">使用計分卡計量時，應該監視服務以確保它們符合商業預期。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-165">Using the scorecard metrics, the services should be monitored to ensure that they meet the business expectations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-166">這表示服務監視是不可或缺的。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-166">This means that service monitoring is essential.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-167">若無法監視一組服務是否符合非功能性需求，則對應的計分卡計量應該考慮以紅色呈現。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-167">If you are not able to monitor a set of services with respect to the non-functional requirements, then the corresponding scorecard metrics should be considered red.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-168">在此案例中，補救措施的第一個步驟是實作適當的服務監視機制。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-168">In this case, the first step for remediation is to implement the appropriate service monitoring.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="d415c-169">例如，若公司預期服務以 99.99% 的可用性運作，但沒有可用的生產遙測可測量可用性，您應該假設您不符合該需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-169">For example, if the business expects a service to operate with 99.99% availability, but there is no production telemetry in place to measure the availability, you should assume that you're not meeting the requirement.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d415c-170">**規劃補救措施**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-170">**Plan remediation**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-171">針對計量低於可接受閾值的每個服務作業，判斷補救服務成本以讓作業達到接受的計量。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-171">For each service operation with metrics that fall below an acceptable threshold, determine the cost of remediating the service to bring operation to an acceptable metric.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-172">若補救服務成本大於服務預期產生的收益，請接著考慮無形成本，例如客戶體驗。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-172">If the cost of remediating the service is greater than the expected revenue generation of the service, move on to consider the non-tangible costs such as customer experience.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d415c-173">例如，若客戶難以使用服務成功下訂，他們可能會選擇競爭者服務。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d415c-173">For example, if customers have difficulty placing a successful order using the service, they may choose a competitor instead.</span></span>
